# Changelog

All notable changes to the *We the Users* founding documents and supporting materials.

Versioning follows the scheme in `build/pdf/versions.json`: pre-launch drafts are v0.x
(the minor bumps on each substantive change), and every founding document becomes
**v1.0 on 2026-07-02** — launch day — when the set is published. Per-document current
versions and dates are mirrored in `VERSIONS.md`, regenerated by `build/pdf/check_meta.py`.
This file is the narrative history; `VERSIONS.md` is the current-state snapshot.

## 2026-07-02 — Launch day

- **Launched wetheusers.ai**: draft gate removed, site public, share card and launch metadata live.
- **Added the sixth essay** — *The Second Zero* (v0.1), the keystone: the two cost collapses, the destination, and the commonwealth's economics — revenue funds the government (builders and moderators as a salaried civil service), and profits divide strictly equally, one person, one share, gated on verifiable personhood.
- Renamed essay 02 to *Your Digital Shadow*; retitled essay 01's closing bargain line; added the second first believer.

## [Unreleased] — heading toward v1.0 (launch 2026-07-02)

### Added
- **Founding brief** — the full argument, structure, and open questions (14 pp), now
  with its key empirical claim and the ideas it builds on credited in source footnotes.
- **Declaration of Internet Independence** — the statement to be published on July 2;
  epigraph from Federalist No. 1.
- **Five essays** making the public case: *We Never Agreed*, *Your Digital Shadow*, *Ambition Against Ambition* (epigraph from Federalist No. 51),
  *The Longer Race* (the economic argument), and *The Ledger and the Spoils* (why crypto
  decentralized the ledger but not the rewards, and how AI and crypto together can) —
  each now carrying source footnotes, with every claim checked against live sources.
- **Economics research note** with every load-bearing and supporting source verified
  against live sources, now threaded through as footnotes in the same style as the rest.
- **Framer-invitation packet** — ten pieces, all PDF, in reading order from a one-page
  "Start here" through the brief.
- **Launch deck** (`deck/`) — a 19-slide public launch-and-join presentation (`.pptx`), built by `deck/build_deck.js`, with a slide for the essence of each of the five essays.
- **Launch website** (`website/index.html`) — a self-contained, persuasive single-page site in the “nocturnal charter” design, with bespoke inline-SVG diagrams and a full-text reader for the Declaration and all five essays (drop caps, footnotes, readable tables, prev/next, deep links). Built by `website/build_site.py`, which pulls the document text straight from `/docs`. Sign-on is a front-end preview pending hosting.
- **Authorship on the website**: a “first signer” section before the sign-on (plain-language lead, with an expandable, linked track record — ArrayFire, Intel, oneAPI, the UXL Foundation — and an independence note), and John Melonakos's signature threaded into the foot of the Declaration in the reader, in place of the founding-signers placeholder.
- **Version/date machinery**: a single source of truth (`versions.json`), a stamp
  generator the render scripts read from (`docmeta.py`), and a validator that checks every
  rendered PDF against the registry and regenerates `VERSIONS.md` (`check_meta.py`).
- **Build reproducibility**: pinned Python dependencies in `build/pdf/requirements.txt`.

### Changed
- **Locked the project vocabulary**: the Commonwealth (the whole federation and its top
  tier), Communities, Individuals, the Covenant (the constitution), "We the Users" as the
  public banner, and "collaboration / collaborative" as the economic language. The proper
  working name was retired everywhere except the founding brief's Appendix F.9, where it is
  documented as a candidate name.
- **Threaded the thousand-year horizon** through the brief, the Declaration, and
  *The Longer Race*.
- **Replaced the old frozen-date convention** with the registry-driven system above, so
  each document's printed date reflects its last substantive change. The economics note's
  in-text "Last touched" date is now driven by the registry as well, leaving no
  hand-maintained dates in the documents.
- **Threaded the central wordplay “We the Users → we the used”** through the materials at each one’s point of indictment: the Declaration’s grievances, the close of the brief’s case and of *We Never Agreed*, deck slide 3 and the closing turn, and the website’s problem section.
- **Renamed the repository root** from its original codename to `wetheusers/` to match the domain wetheusers.ai, repointing the hardcoded build-script paths.
