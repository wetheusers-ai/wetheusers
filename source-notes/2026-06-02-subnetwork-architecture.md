# Founder note — the subnetwork middle layer and division of powers
*Typed input, June 2, 2026. Given in response to the question of how to reconcile "capture holistic digital shadows" with consent and the anti-surveillance principles. Folded into founding-brief.md §3.*

The entire point of the middle layer of subnetworks is to hammer out these issues, while holding true to the principles and ideals of the broader collaboration. Every individual has a data store, sacred to them. They ultimately have complete control over how they choose to share their data. They can choose to walk away from the network altogether, and the network will release them but also revoke their membership privileges. This can happen at the subnetwork-level, and at the global network level. The global network will define which summary statistics each subnetwork must share and how (i.e., the intra-network permissions and sub-network membership requirements). Each subnetwork can decide how to filter incoming messages, how to present ADs to their network (still the revenue source for the whole global and sub-network business), and how much revenue to pass on to their participants. The global network takes some percentage fee to build and run the software project on which it all runs. But within subnetworks, individuals have a great deal of control over who they share their data with and whether they agree to contribute their data to building the AI which gives the company its competitive edge over the non-collaborative competitors.
