# An Invitation to the Framers

*Part of **We the Users***

---

## The short version

On **July 2, 2026** — the 250th anniversary of the vote for American independence — we will publish a **Declaration of Internet Independence**: a statement that the internet should belong to the people who use it, and that we intend to build it. What follows the declaration is the real work — writing, in public, the **constitution** of a user-owned internet. We are inviting a small group of **framers** to help lead that work, and we would like you to be one of them.

## Why now

Two forces make this the moment. The internet has consolidated into a handful of owners who capture the value that billions of people create. And artificial intelligence, trained on the digital shadows of humanity, is rapidly compounding that advantage in the hands of those who already hold the data. The terms of the next era of the internet are being set right now; they can be set *for* people or *to* them. We mean to settle them, in Hamilton's phrase, "from reflection and choice" rather than "accident and force."

## What a framer is asked to do

- **Deliberate the hard questions in the open.** The founding brief lays out the genuine unresolved questions — data that belongs to more than one person, governance and speech, how power is kept from re-concentrating, how the network is funded and bootstrapped, the legal landscape. Framers help work these out in public.
- **Lend judgment, and a name.** Your expertise, and your willingness to stand behind the effort, give it seriousness and credibility.
- **Help moderate the public process.** The constitution will be argued in the open; framers help keep that debate rigorous, fair, and honest.

This is a founding role, not an advisory title. The commitment is real but bounded — we will be explicit about time and scope as we convene.

## Who we hope to gather

People who have given themselves to a freer and fairer digital world: open-source builders and maintainers, digital-rights advocates, scholars of constitutions and governance, economists of data and platforms, and serious builders in decentralized and Web3 systems. We care about judgment and conviction more than titles.

## What is already in hand

- **The founding brief** — the full vision, the architecture, the historical model, and every open question, candidly stated.
- **A first draft of the Declaration** — the July 2 centerpiece.
- **A series of five short essays** making the public case — on the broad argument, your data, how power is divided, the economics, and how AI and crypto fit.

(All of it can travel with this invitation.)
